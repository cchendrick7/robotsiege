Robot Siege — Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 22, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how the mobile game Robot Siege ("the app", "we", "us") handles information. By using the app you agree to this policy.

The short version

Robot Siege has no ads and does not sell your data or track you across other apps. There is no account to create. We use Apple's Game Center for optional leaderboards, Apple's iCloud to sync your saved game across your devices, and a crash-reporting tool to help us fix bugs.

Information we collect

We do not ask for your name, email address, location, or contacts. The limited data involved is:

Crash diagnostics. If the app crashes, an automated crash report (such as the crash type, your device model, iOS version, and app version) is sent to our crash-reporting provider, Sentry, so we can diagnose and fix the problem. These reports are technical only, are not used to identify or track you, and carry no name or personal identifier.

Game Center (optional). If you use leaderboards, Apple's Game Center associates your Game Center player ID with your scores (for example, the highest wave you reach on each map and difficulty). This is handled by Apple under Apple's terms; you can disable Game Center in your device Settings.

Game data & storage

Your progress (levels, tokens, upgrades, settings, best waves) is stored locally on your device. If you are signed in to iCloud, a copy is also kept in your own iCloud account so your progress follows you across your devices. That iCloud data is managed by Apple, not by us, and we cannot read it. Deleting the app removes the local copy from your device.

In-app purchases

Robot Siege offers optional in-app purchases. All payments are processed by Apple through the App Store. We never receive or store your payment details. Apple's handling of your purchase information is governed by Apple's Privacy Policy.

Third-party services

The app does not include advertising or cross-app tracking SDKs. The third parties involved are: Apple (App Store purchases, Game Center leaderboards, and iCloud save sync), and Sentry (crash reporting), each as described above. Sentry's handling of crash data is governed by Sentry's Privacy Policy.

Children's privacy

Robot Siege is suitable for a general audience and does not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. We do not collect names, contacts, or similar personal details from anyone. Crash diagnostics are technical and are not used to identify any user, and Game Center is operated by Apple under its own terms.

Changes to this policy

If the app's data practices change (for example, if crash reporting or Game Center features are added in a future update), we will update this policy and revise the "Last updated" date above.
